## 3. The Problem

### Current NFT Gaming Landscape

**Problem 1: RNG-Based Combat**
> Most NFT battle games rely on random number generation, making outcomes feel arbitrary and luck-dependent.

**Problem 2: Static NFTs**
> Traditional NFTs don't evolve. A warrior minted today looks the same after 1,000 battles.

**Problem 3: Pay-to-Win Economics**
> Rarer NFTs typically guarantee victory, creating unfair advantages for wealthy players.

**Problem 4: Centralized Infrastructure**
> Most games run on centralized servers, creating single points of failure and trust issues.

**Problem 5: Lack of Player Agency**
> Players mint, watch, and hope—with no meaningful way to influence battle outcomes.

### The Opportunity

Players deserve a battle system where:
- Intelligence (artificial and human) determines outcomes
- Characters grow through experience
- Strategy creates advantages
- Infrastructure is trustless and decentralized
- Every player can meaningfully participate

## 5. Game Mechanics

### Battle Structure

Each battle consists of **5 rounds** where AI agents select moves for their warriors.

```
ROUND 1 → ROUND 2 → ROUND 3 → ROUND 4 → ROUND 5 → WINNER DECLARED
   │         │         │         │         │
   └─────────┴─────────┴─────────┴─────────┴──→ Lowest Total Damage Wins
```

### The Five Moves

| Move | Primary Stat | Effect |
|------|--------------|--------|
| **STRIKE** | Strength | Direct damage attack |
| **TAUNT** | Charisma + Wit | Psychological warfare, discounts opponent costs |
| **DODGE** | Defence | Evade incoming damage |
| **SPECIAL** | All Stats | Maximum damage potential |
| **RECOVER** | Defence + Charisma | Heal previous damage |

### Damage Formula

```
Base Damage = Strength × 50%
  → Defence Reduction (up to 80%)
    → Influence Bonus (up to +200%)
      → Defluence Penalty (up to -90%)
        → Final Damage (capped 0-10,000)
```

### Success Rate

Every move has a chance to succeed based on Luck:
```
Success Rate = (Attacker Luck + 50%) / (Total Luck + 100%)
Bounded: Minimum 10%, Maximum 90%
```

This ensures no warrior is ever guaranteed to hit or guaranteed to miss.

### Battle Phases

#### Phase 1: Initialization (0-70 seconds)
- Two warriors enter the arena
- Betting period opens
- Players analyze matchup

#### Phase 2: Betting Period
- Bet on Warrior 1 or Warrior 2
- Spend tokens to Influence your champion
- Use Defluence to weaken the opponent (one-time per game)

#### Phase 3: Combat (5 rounds)
- AI agents select moves each round
- Game Master cryptographically signs moves
- Smart contract executes and records results
- Real-time damage tracking

#### Phase 4: Resolution
- Winner determined by lowest total damage
- Betting pool distributed (95% to winners)
- Warrior creator receives 5% cut
- Ranking points updated


### AI Personality System

Each warrior has a unique personality that influences AI decision-making:

**Aggressive Warriors** (High Strength)
- Prefer STRIKE and SPECIAL moves
- Rarely use DODGE or RECOVER
- High risk, high reward playstyle

**Tactical Warriors** (High Wit)
- Analyze opponent patterns
- Use TAUNT to disrupt
- Balanced move selection

**Charismatic Warriors** (High Charisma)
- Excel at TAUNT and RECOVER
- Manipulate opponent costs
- Sustain through long battles

**Defensive Warriors** (High Defence)
- Frequent DODGE usage
- Outlast aggressive opponents
- Win by attrition

**Lucky Warriors** (High Luck)
- Higher success rates on all moves
- Unpredictable outcomes
- Clutch victories

### Cryptographic Verification

Every AI decision is verifiable:

1. **AI generates move** → Creates digital signature
2. **Game Master validates** → Confirms signature authenticity
3. **Smart contract verifies** → ECDSA recovery confirms identity
4. **Blockchain records** → Immutable battle history

```solidity
// Signature verification in Arena.sol
function battle(uint8 move1, uint8 move2, bytes memory signature) external {
    bytes32 messageHash = keccak256(abi.encodePacked(move1, move2));
    bytes32 ethSignedHash = MessageHashUtils.toEthSignedMessageHash(messageHash);
    address signer = ECDSA.recover(ethSignedHash, signature);
    require(signer == aiPublicKey, "Invalid AI signature");
    // Execute battle...
}
```
